Cura the Horse Runs With Her Hero Through Village Streets

  • tweet
  • EMAIL
  • tweet
  • By: Laura S.
  • February 25, 2016
  • About Laura

Police in Nis, Serbia, called Dušan Stojanović for help when they needed expert help catching a horse who had escaped her abusers and was running frantically through the streets. Months later Dušan and the horse are still running, but for very different reasons.

The horse, now named Cura (which means girl) ran away from her abusive Roma “owners” in Serbia. When Dušan brought her to his rescue center called Zoo Planet, he began to assess the most visible signs of neglect. He began helping her put weight on and removed the many painful burs from her head. The scars from belts and ropes on her chest tell a small piece of her history as a towing horse. But it’s her strongly defensive behavior that says the most. So far, Cura appears to find the greatest comfort in simply running. So every day, even when the wind blows too cold against his chest, Dušan sets out with her on a run through the village. The momentum and exertion sooth Cura and settle her down just enough to allow a select few volunteers to handle her for feeding and grooming.

Two days ago, Dušan had a very big scare when Cura’s original “owners” came to his rescue center to demand the return of their horse.

“The owners have surfaced and want to sell the mare to the butchers,” Goran said in a distress call to the Harmony Fund international animal rescue charity. “This is unacceptable for us. We are stuck. On the one hand, we have a mare which we don’t have money to take care of but on the other hand, we can’t let the owners take her to the butcher’s. The only solution that we have is to buy out the mare from the owners. For this, we would need 700 Euros (about $777 American dollars). Once we buy out the mare, we will find her a home where she can grow old. She is really beautiful, healthy, young and strong girl and what happened with her is terrible. We need the money in next 7 days to realize this activity and save her from the butchers. Do you think you could help?”

The Harmony Fund shared Cura’s story on Facebook and raised funds to make sure that Cura would have her happily-ever-after. But for now she’ll remain with Dušan as he’s trying to arrange for special help with her hooves.

“We are managing to comb her mane and to groom her, but we need to cut the hooves. They are now growing fast,” Dusan said. “This is still a big problem because she won’t allow to us to touch her hooves. We are trying to collect money for this and to find somebody who has a frame for horse hooves treatments and who can do this.”
