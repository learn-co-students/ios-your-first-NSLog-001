Serial stowaway Marilyn Hartman arrested again at Chicago's O'Hare Airport

David Boroff · Feb 18, 2016 10:08 AM

Apparently the nursing home was not exciting enough for her.

Serial stowaway Marilyn Hartman, who was arrested five times in airplane-related incidents last year, was busted again on Wednesday at Chicago's O'Hare Airport.


The 64-year-old Hartman was arrested at approximately 2:30 p.m. at an airport bus shuttle center, Chicago cops said.

Hartman has attempted many times in recent years to board planes without a ticket, and succeeded on occasion.

She had been released on probation to a nursing home in Chicago in December, and ordered to avoid airports and Chicago's railroad station.

She is charged this time with violating her probation and criminal trespass. Hartman will appear in bond court on Thursday.

Called a "habitual trespasser" by cops, Hartman was arrested twice in as many days last year in Chicago.

She claims she successfully sneaked about a Southwest Airlines flight from San Jose to Los Angeles two years ago. She also said she once flew from Minneapolis to Florida without a ticket.


An attempt to fly to Hawaii from San Francisco was thwarted when the actual ticket holder showed up, and Hartman was booted before takeoff.


She has said that homelessness has driven her to take "desperate measures." She says she feels safer in airports.
