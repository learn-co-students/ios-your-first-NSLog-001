The five stages of moving out of home

Feb 12, 2016 Madeleine Wedesweiler
Based on 'serious analysis' there are five separate stages of moving out of home. Photo: Stocksy

Moving out of home is rarely a clear-cut process and depending on your level of ability to cope in the real world, it may take you several attempts to successfully complete this rite of passage.

Based on ‘serious analysis’, I have discovered there are five key stages of moving out. Cut yourself some slack if you haven’t cleared the first stages though, ‘adulting’ can be hard.

1. The scheming begins

You don’t think you can take another day of living with your family. Your dad keeps making bad jokes, your sister’s wearing your clothes again and if your mum asks you one more time when you’re going to get a real job, swear to god…

You’re subtly trying to get yourself kicked out so they pay for you to rent somewhere else, and it’s almost working. Every night you dream of sweet, sweet freedom and wake up to the sound of vacuuming instead. Or maybe you want to move out with your partner and you’re dreaming of them bringing you breakfast in bed and giving you a foot rub. How naive.

2. (Over) Staying at a friend or partner’s place

This stage is kind of like moving out. It’s amazing at first, it’s like a super fun sleepover club where you stay up each night, eating whole bags of lollies and playing truth or dare and talking about who you have a crush on.

But to nobody’s great surprise the fun runs out. Your interactions with the legitimate residents get more and more passive-aggressive until you get asked to contribute to the rent. See, you ate someone’s organic yoghurt with their name written on it one too many times. “This isn’t some kind of halfway house where you can do whatever you want,” they say. Ugh! fine.

Staying at a friend’s place is pretty great until you get asked to pay one night’s rent. Video: Bondi Hipsters/YouTube.

3. Moving into your first share house

Welcome to freedom, baby, you’re a tenant at a provisional level.

Though you’ve mustered up enough initiative to borrow a station-wagon and dump all of your detritus in it, you haven’t gotten too ahead of yourself just yet. You’re still dropping in on your family to use their internet and washing machine, and your freezer is full of Tupperware containers of mum’s lasagne. You keep going back there for a number of reasons; you may be poor, unorganised, or your housemates are mad at you for turning every Thursday night into a party.

You keep starting house parties but your housemates aren’t into it.

4. Renting like a boss

You now have your life together, well mostly. You’ve moved to some place nice, where all the windows actually open and if you climb out onto the roof you get a waterfront view. It’s not important that it’s of someone else’s swimming pool, it’s still a waterfront view.

You’re doing grown up tasks like paying your own health insurance and ironing your shirts and you, the top dog, have the biggest bedroom in the house. But you’re not quite there yet. You’re still paying up to 38 per cent of your income on rent and you’re yet to use the oven.

5. Independence Day

So you’ve just put a deposit down on a home.

This is the most advanced stage of the process. A full ‘good on ya mate’ and a bottle of champagne to you if you’ve reached this stage, you’re basically a genius. You probably have your own business cards printed, or at least an official-looking email signature. Though it does have one not insignificant downside: you’re chained to a mortgage until you die, or pay it off, whichever comes first.
